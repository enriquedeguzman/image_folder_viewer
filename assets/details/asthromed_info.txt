Contents Aspirin

Indications/Uses Prophylaxis of cerebrovascular disease or MI for the
secondary prevention of thrombotic cerebrovascular or CV disease. Pain
in musculoskeletal conditions, headache, dysmenorrhea, pyrexia & fever.
Click to view Asthromed detailed prescribing information

Dosage/Direction for Use Analgesic/antipyretic 300-900 mg every 4-6 hr.
Max of 4 g/day. Prevention of MI & stroke 75-100 mg. 

Administration Should be taken with food: Take immediately after meals.
Swallow whole, do not chew/crush.

Contraindications Hypersensitivity to aspirin & other NSAIDs. History or
active peptic ulcer. Hemophilia, gout. Children, adolescents <16 yr &
lactation (risk of Reye’s syndrome). 

Special Precautions Patients susceptible to dyspepsia or w/ gastric
mucosal lesion. 

Adverse Reactions Nausea, dyspepsia, vomiting; urticaria & other skin
eruptions, angioedema, rhinitis, severe or even fatal paroxysmal
bronchospasm & dyspnea especially w/ asthma, chronic urticaria or
chronic rhinitis. Increased bleeding time, decreased platelet
adhesiveness & in large doses, hypoprothrombinemia; salicylism. Reye’s
syndrome (in children).

Drug Interactions Enhanced effect on the GIT w/ alcohol. Enhanced
activity of coumarin anticoagulants, sulfonylureas, hypoglycemic agents,
methotrexate, phenytoin & valproic acid. Diminished effects of
uricosurics eg, probenecid & sulfinpyrazone.
