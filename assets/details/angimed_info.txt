Contents Trimetazidine HCl

Indications/Uses Preventive treatment of episodes of angina pectoris.
Adjuvant symptomatic treatment of vertigo & tinnitus. Adjuvant treatment
of visual disorders of circulatory origin. Click to view Angimed
detailed prescribing information

Dosage/Direction for Use 1 tab in the morning & evening. Click to view
Angimed detailed prescribing information

Overdosage View Angimed overdosage for action to be taken in the event
of an overdose.

Administration Should be taken with food: Swallow whole w/ glass of
water.

Contraindications Hypersensitivity. Parkinson’s disease, parkinsonian
symptoms, tremors, restless leg syndrome, & other related movement
disorder. Severe renal impairment (CrCl <30 mL/min). Not recommended
during breastfeeding. Click to view Angimed detailed prescribing
information

Special Precautions Discontinue use on occurrence of movement disorder
eg, parkinsonian symptoms, restless leg syndrome, tremors, gait
instability. Not a curative treatment for angina attacks, nor an initial
treatment for unstable angina pectoris or MI, nor in the pre-hospital
phase or during the 1st days of hospitalization. Can aggravate or cause
symptoms similar to those of Parkinson’s disease. Falls may occur
following a drop in BP or loss of balance, particularly in patients
taking antihypertensives. Patients w/ moderate renal impairment. Not to
be taken during pregnancy. Elderly >75 yr. Click to view Angimed
detailed prescribing information

Use In Pregnancy & Lactation Click to view Angimed detailed prescribing
information

Adverse Reactions Dizziness, headache, abdominal pain, diarrhea,
dyspepsia, nausea, vomiting, rash, pruritus, urticaria, asthenia. Click
to view Angimed detailed prescribing information

Drug Interactions Not to be co-administered w/ MAOIs. Click to view
Angimed detailed prescribing information
